7:00 – 7:30 am
Breakfast
Food
 Start with a light breakfast near your hotel — congee, youtiao (fried dough sticks), or a jianbing crepe from a street cart. Keep it quick — the Temple of Heaven park is best enjoyed early before tour groups arrive.
7:30 – 10:30 am
Temple of Heaven
Historical
Built in 1420, this was where Ming and Qing emperors performed sacred rituals each winter solstice to pray for good harvests — believing themselves to be the divine link between heaven and earth. Its circular design embodies Chinese cosmology: heaven is round, earth is square. UNESCO World Heritage Site.
 Arrive early to watch locals in the surrounding park doing Tai Chi, water calligraphy, singing opera, and playing Chinese chess. Then visit the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests and walk the Echo Wall and Circular Mound Altar. Buy the all-inclusive ticket to access all inner structures. The park opens at 6am — earlier entry means more local life to observe.
10:30 – 11:00 am
Transfer to Summer Palace
Scenic
 Take a Didi northwest across the city to the Summer Palace — about 20–25 minutes depending on traffic. Book the Didi in advance or ask your hotel to arrange a car for a fixed price if you prefer.
11:00 am – 2:00 pm
Summer Palace
Scenic Historical
Originally built in 1750 by Emperor Qianlong as a royal retreat, the Summer Palace was ransacked and burned by Anglo-French forces in 1860, then rebuilt by Empress Dowager Cixi who controversially diverted navy funds for its restoration. It stands as both a masterpiece of imperial garden design and a symbol of China's turbulent 19th century. UNESCO World Heritage Site.
 Stroll the famous Long Corridor (728m of painted galleries), take a boat on Kunming Lake, and climb Longevity Hill for panoramic views over the water and surrounding parkland. One of the most beautiful imperial gardens in China. Buy tickets online to skip the queue. A rowing boat on the lake is a lovely way to spend 30–40 minutes. Wear sunscreen — the lakeside is very exposed.
2:00 – 3:00 pm
Lunch near the palace
Food
 Several restaurants just outside the Summer Palace east gate serve good Cantonese and Beijing-style dishes. Or pack a simple picnic to eat by the lake. Avoid the overpriced stalls inside the palace grounds — better value and quality just outside the gates.
3:00 – 6:00 pm
798 Art District
Modern
 About 30 min by Didi east. Wander through decommissioned Bauhaus-style factory buildings now filled with contemporary galleries, sculpture gardens, design studios, independent bookshops, and street art. A fascinating window into China's modern creative scene. Entry to the district is free — individual galleries may charge a small fee. UCCA Center for Contemporary Art is the standout venue. Afternoons are the liveliest time to visit.
6:00 pm onwards
Evening options
Option A Option B
Option A — Sanlitun
Dinner + bar district
Head to Sanlitun for dinner at 6:00pm — Haidilao for hot pot, Jing Yaa Tang for upscale Beijing cuisine, or the many Sichuan and international restaurants around Taikoo Li. Then explore Beijing's premier nightlife strip: rooftop bars, craft beer spots, live music, and cocktail lounges all within walking distance. Things get lively from 9pm onwards.
Best for: a lively, cosmopolitan night out. Very international crowd, easy to navigate.
Option B — Peking Opera
Dinner near Qianmen + Liyuan Theatre
Dine at 6:00pm near Qianmen — Bianyifang (est. 1416, one of Beijing's oldest Peking Duck restaurants) is an excellent choice and right by the theatre. At 7:30pm attend a traditional Peking Opera performance at the Liyuan Theatre inside the Qianmen Hotel — elaborate costumes, face paint, acrobatics, and live orchestra. Shows run about 1.5 hours with English subtitles. Optional nightcap at Shichahai lake (10 min by Didi) afterwards.
Best for: a uniquely Beijing cultural experience. Book Liyuan tickets in advance — performances sell out. Bianyifang is a great alternative to Quanjude for Peking Duck.
