Time Stop What to do Tips
6:30 – 7:00 am
Early departure
Scenic
 Leave Beijing early by private car or Didi. The drive to Mutianyu takes about 1.5 hrs — through the city, then into forested Huairou hills. Grab a quick hotel breakfast or pick up pastries on the way. Leaving by 6:30am gets you to the wall by 8am, well ahead of tour groups which typically arrive from 9:30am onwards. This makes a huge difference to the experience.
8:00 – 8:30 am
Arrive Mutianyu
Historical
Construction of the Great Wall began over 2,000 years ago under Emperor Qin Shi Huang, with the Mutianyu section built during the Northern Qi dynasty (550–577 AD) and later reinforced by the Ming dynasty in the 1500s. Stretching over 21,000 km in total, it is the longest man-made structure ever built — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the great wonders of the world.
 Buy tickets at the entrance. Choose the cable car (gondola) up to the wall — this saves your legs for hiking along the ridge rather than the steep climb from the base. Bring passports — required for entry. Buy the combined ticket (entrance + cable car up + toboggan down) for the best value. Pack water and sunscreen.
8:30 – 11:30 am
Great Wall hike
Hiking
 Hike east from Tower 6 to Tower 23 — the most scenic and well-preserved stretch at Mutianyu. The path follows a dramatic ridgeline with forested hills on both sides. You can push further west towards the wilder unrestored sections for more solitude if energy allows. The wall is uneven underfoot — proper shoes are essential. The eastern towers have the best views and the least foot traffic. Allow about 3 hours for a comfortable moderate pace with stops for photos.
11:30 am – 12:00 pm
Toboggan descent
Activity
 Take the famous stainless steel toboggan slide back down the mountain — a 1,580m run through the forest. You control your own speed with a hand brake. One of the most fun things you can do in Beijing. Queues build up from 11am — doing this before noon keeps the wait short. Go at a relaxed pace and enjoy it; the ride takes about 5–8 minutes.
12:00 – 1:30 pm
Lunch — Huairou village
Food
 Drive 10 minutes down to Huairou village for lunch at a local restaurant. Order hand-pulled noodles, braised pork belly, local mountain trout, and stir-fried wild vegetables — hearty northern Chinese countryside cooking at very reasonable prices. Ask your driver to recommend a spot locals use — they'll know. Avoid the tourist-facing restaurants right at the wall entrance which are overpriced and mediocre.
1:30 – 2:00 pm
Mutianyu village stroll
Scenic
 Take a short walk through the old stone village at the base of the wall — traditional courtyard houses, persimmon trees, and a slower pace of rural Beijing life. A lovely contrast to the city. Small stalls sell locally grown walnuts, chestnuts, and dried fruit — good snacks for the drive back.
2:00 – 3:30 pm
Return to Beijing
Scenic
 Drive back through the Huairou valley — the forested hills and old reservoir views make for a scenic return journey. Drop your bags at the hotel and freshen up. Traffic into Beijing can build from 4pm on weekdays. Leaving Mutianyu by 2pm avoids the worst of it.
4:00 – 6:30 pm
Nanluoguxiang hutongs
Scenic
 Spend the late afternoon in Nanluoguxiang — one of Beijing's best-preserved hutong neighbourhoods. Browse independent shops, indie cafes, and small galleries tucked into the alleyways. A more relaxed end to an active day. Nanluoguxiang itself can be touristy — duck into the side hutongs branching off it (Mao'er Hutong, Banchang Hutong) for a more authentic feel.
6:30 – 8:00 pm
Dinner
Food
 Try Sichuan cuisine tonight for a change of pace — Mapo tofu, dan dan noodles, and spicy hot pot. Several excellent Sichuan restaurants are clustered around the Gulou (Drum Tower) area, a short walk from Nanluoguxiang. After a day of walking, a warming Sichuan hot pot is one of the great Beijing evening experiences. Great for sharing between two people.
8:00 pm onwards
Gulou bar street
Nightlife
 The Drum Tower (Gulou) neighbourhood has a excellent selection of low-key bars, craft beer spots, and rooftop terraces. Much more local and relaxed than Sanlitun — perfect for a quieter night after a full day outdoors. Great Leap Brewing is a Beijing institution with a beautiful courtyard location nearby. Several rooftop bars offer views of the illuminated Drum Tower itself.
